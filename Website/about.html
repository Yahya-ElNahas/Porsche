Mission:

Porsche, the German automotive manufacturer, has a mission centered around engineering excellence, innovation, and performance. While it does not have an explicitly stated mission statement, the essence of Porsche's mission revolves around several key principles:

  • Engineering Excellence: Porsche is committed to creating vehicles that exemplify precision engineering, quality craftsmanship, and cutting-edge technology. They strive for excellence in design, performance, and functionality.

  • Innovation: Porsche is dedicated to pushing the boundaries of automotive technology and design. They continuously innovate to improve their vehicles' performance, efficiency, and safety while staying true to their brand heritage.
  • Passion for Driving: Porsche is passionate about driving pleasure and the thrill of the road. Their mission involves creating vehicles that deliver an exhilarating driving experience, whether on the racetrack or the open road.
  • Heritage and Tradition: Porsche values its rich history and tradition of automotive excellence. They aim to honor their legacy while embracing modern advancements to remain at the forefront of the automotive industry.
  • Overall, Porsche's mission is to create exceptional sports cars that embody the brand's values of performance, innovation, and driving enjoyment, while also maintaining a commitment to quality and craftsmanship.

    24 Hours of Le Mans success in the 70s :

    Porsche does not only have a history in Formula 1 however, it also made a name for itself in various other motorsport categories. Its greatest success was undoubtedly in the 24 Hours of Le Mans. Porsche is still the record holder with eighteen victories. The brand's first victory came with the Porsche 917 in 1970, after which a very successful decade began. Porsche's cars continued to dominate the sport in the 1970s, with a string of victories to their name. It was a prelude to a successful future.

    Late 60s in Monte Carlo Rally :

    Porsche had already proved itself in the field of rallies. At the end of the 1960s, it dominated the Monte Carlo Rally. No less than three seasons in a row, it managed to take a double victory, thereby keeping the competition behind it.

    Vic Elford and Pauli Toivonen first managed a double victory for Porsche in 1968, followed by Björn Waldegard and Garard Larrousse a year later. In 1970, the latter duo also managed to finish on the podium together. It only strengthened Porsche's name in motor racing.

        Powered by a 5.7-liter, 605-horsepower V10 engine originally designed for Le Mans duties,
        the Carrera GT made its debut in the concept form at the 2000 Paris Motor Show
        The Carrera GT went out of production in 2007 and the rare supercar is hugely desirable amongst car collectors.
